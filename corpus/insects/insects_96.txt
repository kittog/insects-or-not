of diurnal moth Native to Northern America.

== Biology ==

=== Morphology ===
The sage brush sheep moth is a beautiful white moth with a wingspan of 71-93 mm.&lt;ref&gt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemileuca_hera&lt;/ref&gt; The white wings have black markings on their subtle wings including a small black crescent on each wings alongsides black stripes. The moth has fluffy orange bodies with black and white markings present on the thorax. Males have feathery crimson antennae. 

=== Behaviour ===

==== Imago behaviour ====
Imagos do not feed and are diurnal by nature. Emergence from the occurs during early morning hours. The adults can be observed from July to September&lt;ref&gt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemileuca_hera&lt;/ref&gt; but varies depending on location. The adults are known to fly several meters above the ground and have an erratic flight patterns.&lt;ref&gt;http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/browse/family-saturniidae/subfamily-hemileucinae/hemileuca/hemileuca-hera/&lt;/ref&gt;

==== Infant behaviour ====
Infants feed in groups when young, as they mature they tend to become more solitary in nature.&lt;ref&gt;http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/browse/family-saturniidae/subfamily-hemileucinae/hemileuca/hemileuca-hera/&lt;/ref&gt;

=== Life cycle ===
The females lay their eggs on sagebrush plant in a ring shape&lt;ref&gt;https://www.butterfliesandmoths.org/species/Hemileuca-hera&lt;/ref&gt; on the surface of the leaves. The infants hatch during mid summers to mid late spring. The caterpillars build cocoons in mulch and leaf litter&lt;ref&gt;http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/browse/family-saturniidae/subfamily-hemileucinae/hemileuca/hemileuca-hera/&lt;/ref&gt; followed by the emergence of adults during early springtime.

=== Ecology ===

==== Host plants&lt;ref&gt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemileuca_hera&lt;/ref&gt; ====

* Big basin sagebrush 
* Sand sagebrush 
* threetip sagebrush 
* Bluebonnets (Lupine)
* Wild buckwheat 
* Sulphurflower buckwheat&lt;ref&gt;https://bugguide.net/node/view/61936&lt;/ref&gt;

== Distribution ==

=== Geographical range ===
[[File:Hemileauca Hera(2).jpeg|thumb]]
''Hemileuca hera'' has a vast range over Northern America ranging from Colorado, New Mexico, Arizona, California, San Francisco, Las Vegas and Washington.&lt;ref&gt;https://www.butterfliesandmoths.org/species/Hemileuca-hera&lt;/ref&gt;

=== Habitat ===
Adult moths can be observed in sagebrush meadows, basins, softwood forests, temperate plains and Juniper woodland areas.
