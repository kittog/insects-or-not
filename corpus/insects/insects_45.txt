      <text bytes="694" sha1="a10m9manb4gn9s8ugr8hwaxzgl90ihx" xml:space="preserve">Lycaenidae is the second-largest family of butterflies, with over 6,000 species worldwide, whose members are also called gossamer-winged butterflies. They constitute about 30% of the known butterfly species. The family comprises seven subfamilies, including the blues, the coppers, the hairstreaks, and the harvesters.

Scientific name: Lycaenidae

Order: [[Lepidoptera]]

Family: Lycaenidae; Leach, 1815

Kingdom: Animalia

Superfamily: [[Papilionoidea]]
